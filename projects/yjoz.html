YJOZ

Methods & journey

YJOZ came in with a working web shop and a clear ceiling: mobile traffic was 72% of sessions, but conversion on mobile web was less than a third of desktop. The fix wasn't a responsive skin — it was a native-feeling app shoppers would actually keep on their phone.

The challenge

A checkout flow that worked on desktop fell apart on a 5-inch screen: address forms, coupon fields, shipping toggles, payment selection — all stacked into a scroll nightmare. Plus, push notifications, wish lists, and order tracking were the table stakes the competition already had.

  • Native iOS + Android from a single Flutter codebase
  • Offline-tolerant cart and wishlist with background sync
  • One-tap checkout with saved payment methods
  • Push notifications tied to restock and price drop events
  • Store Listings — App Store + Play Store — submitted end-to-end

The architecture

Flutter app on top of the existing Laravel API, extended with a mobile- first REST surface. Firebase for push notifications and crashlytics. Stripe + Apple Pay + Google Pay wired in behind a single "Pay" button that picks the right one automatically.

What shipped

iOS + Android in 4 months, from wireframes to App Store approval. Launch week pushed 12,000 installs, and mobile conversion rate on checkout jumped from 1.4% (mobile web) to 4.1% (native) in the first 30 days.

The outcome

App-driven revenue now accounts for 51% of total order volume — overtaking desktop in month 3. The admin dashboard is run by a marketing coordinator, not a developer, which was the unspoken goal the whole time.
