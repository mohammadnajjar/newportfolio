UCOFY

A full digital platform managing the entire lifecycle of used cooking oil collection in the UAE. Five integrated applications, real-time driver tracking, IoT sensor integration for tank level monitoring, and a reporting and accountability system built for regulatory scrutiny.

UCOFY

Methods & journey

The UAE regulator mandated that every litre of used cooking oil be tracked from restaurant to recycling facility — with zero gaps. The existing paper process leaked data at every step: missed pickups went unreported, drivers skipped stops, and quantities were rounded to convenience. UCOFY replaced that with a single digital rail.

The challenge

Five different stakeholder groups — regulators, collection company admins, restaurant managers, drivers, and supervisors — each with different needs, different levels of technical comfort, and different devices. One system had to serve all of them without becoming a bloated compromise.

  • Real-time GPS tracking for a fleet of 40+ drivers
  • IoT tank-level sensors sending readings every 60 seconds
  • Multi-role dashboards for regulators, operators, and field staff
  • Offline-tolerant mobile apps — some pickups happen underground
  • Regulator-grade audit trail: every action logged, no gaps allowed

The architecture

Laravel API at the core with MySQL for transactional data, Redis for the real-time layer, and a dedicated queue worker cluster for IoT ingestion. The Flutter mobile apps (driver + supervisor) work fully offline and sync when connectivity returns. Three React dashboards (superadmin, admin, restaurant) share a single design system and an OpenAPI contract that kept frontend and backend teams in lockstep.

What shipped

Nine months from first discovery call to first paying customer on the platform. Today UCOFY serves 1,200+ daily active users across the collection chain, with a sustained uptime above 99.95% and a regulatory compliance score that no paper-based competitor can match.

The outcome

Operations that used to require 3 full-time clerks to reconcile at month-end now run themselves — month-end close went from 5 days to 45 minutes. Regulator audits that used to take a week of prep now ship a signed report in under an hour.
